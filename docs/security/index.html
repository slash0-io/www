security

Trust artifacts, not claims. Everything on this page is checkable.

what slash0 can and cannot see

  • No data path. Your traffic never transits anything we operate. We publish lists; your security groups do the enforcing.
  • No credentials in your account. The hosted tier works through an AWS RAM share you accept - we hold no role, no keys, no access to your resources.
  • What we do see, because AWS shows it to any prefix-list owner: the account IDs that accepted our shares, and which of your security groups reference our lists (resource IDs, not their contents). We use that for exactly one thing: refusing to revoke a share while anything of yours still references it.

the trust surface

What you're actually trusting is the contents of the lists. That surface is kept small and auditable: the pipeline is open source, every range traces to the vendor's official publication with a recorded URL, timestamp, and content hash, ranges are never widened beyond what the vendor published, and every publish is signed.

verify the signature yourself

The feed index carries a detached ed25519 signature (index.json.sig, format keyid:base64). The pinned public key is ec91decd19bce7b9eb1d99748aaf7e99e9a1d702b845c9dc65fecc0425189763 (keyid 96b1d6e2) - published here, in the feed README, and pinned in the hosted tier's own verifier. With stock OpenSSL 3:

# build a PEM for the pinned public key
PUB=ec91decd19bce7b9eb1d99748aaf7e99e9a1d702b845c9dc65fecc0425189763
printf '302a300506032b6570032100%s' "$PUB" | xxd -r -p | openssl base64 \
  | (echo '-----BEGIN PUBLIC KEY-----'; cat; echo '-----END PUBLIC KEY-----') > slash0.pem

# fetch the index and its signature
curl -s https://feed.slash0.io/v1/index.json -o index.json
curl -s https://feed.slash0.io/v1/index.json.sig | cut -d: -f2 \
  | openssl base64 -d -A > index.sig

# verify
openssl pkeyutl -verify -pubin -inkey slash0.pem -rawin \
  -in index.json -sigfile index.sig
# → Signature Verified Successfully

Service documents are covered transitively - the signed index records each document's SHA-256:

# the index's recorded hash for stripe…
grep -A2 '"slug": "stripe"' index.json | grep sha256

# …must match the served document
curl -s https://feed.slash0.io/v1/services/stripe.json | shasum -a 256

threat model, stated plainly

  • Covered: tampering anywhere between our build and your systems - CDN, mirror, cache, or network path. The signature and hash chain fail closed: the hosted tier's reconciler refuses unverified data and keeps serving the last-good lists.
  • Covered: bad vendor data and bad publishes - five independent refusal layers, described in how it works.
  • Not covered by signing: a compromise of the build pipeline itself, which holds the signing key. Mitigations today are the pipeline being fully open source with reviewable history and fixture-tested parsers; moving signing out of CI is on the roadmap as the product matures.

reporting

Found something? Please report it privately via GitHub security advisories - also listed in our security.txt. We take list-correctness reports as seriously as vulnerability reports: a wrong range in an allowlist is the failure mode that matters.
